## Project Statement: First Draft

### Case Study
1. What data sources were used in this work?

   The data source for this project is labour and population data from the United States. It includes data from approximately 30 million Americans and allocated work tasks, as well as randomly generated machine data.

2. What future scenario does it address?

   It seeks to simulate a scenario of what society would be like in a situation where labour tasks are randomly allocated.

3. What argument does the statement make about data and power?

   The statement argued that data and artificial intelligence systems are not neutral. They are determined by political and economic power structures. Whoever controls the data benefits from it.

4. What is the intended impact, and is this included in the statement?

   The intended impact is to encourage people to think critically about artificial intelligence, labour and economic systems. This intention is included in the statement through discussions of neoliberalism, inequality, labour exploitation and alternative economic models.

### Drafting with NotebookLM

A draft project statement by NotebookLM:

I am creating a dynamic, interactive visualisation that transforms tourism statistics into a visual narrative about global movement to New Zealand. My work consists of two integrated components: a world map at the top and a line graph below. On the map, I use circles and pie charts to represent visitors from major countries, with different colours indicating their purpose—specifically business, holiday, or visiting friends and relatives. Using p5.js, I have designed this to be an interactive experience where you can use a time slider to see how these patterns shift month-by-month over a total of 36 periods.

The data for this project is drawn from official **Stats NZ** tourism records, alongside geographical central location tables and world map data. Specifically, I am analysing international arrivals from February 2023 through January 2026. While the project focuses on these recent trends, it also looks forward to a **future scenario** where rising global oil prices may drastically alter the number of visitors from our primary source countries.

In developing this work, I am engaging **critically** with how we represent numbers, moving beyond "simply displaying numbers" toward creating "**meaningful narratives**" through the combination of data and text. I want to challenge the reliance on traditional, static charts by using visualisation as a medium for conveying social behaviours and patterns. By allowing you to select individual countries and filter data, the work explores the social or cultural factors that influence tourist behaviour.

Ultimately, I intend for this visualisation to let the public appreciate the **data’s impact** more intuitively. I want you to be able to explore the data for yourselves, uncovering deeper insights into New Zealand’s tourism industry that professionals can use to design targeted services and marketing strategies.
